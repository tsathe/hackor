IDEA. PROTOTPYE.  REPEAT. 

A hackathon is an event in which people at all levels of training (attendings, residents, medical students), and across variety of disciplines (medicine, engineering, design, etc.) come together to come up with problems in a particular field and design solutions through iterative prototyping.

We want to apply this concept to the field of surgery.

TRACK ONE

DEVICE  DESIGN 

One of the essential challenges in surgery is to design instruments that are effective, durable, biologically compatible, and practical. Whether it is making precise incisions, controlling bleeding, improving visual fields, or connecting incised tissue, there are a wide-variety of tasks that must be accomplished during an operation. In this track, we encourage participants to create novel ideas for tools that would improve outcomes for common challenges faced within the operating room.

Prize 1

TRACK TWO

PATIENT  EXPERIENCE 

While a majority of focus is often spent on improving surgical technology, a key component of the surgical experience takes place outside of the operating room. From diagnosis, to admission, and ultimately follow up, the physical and emotional process of undergoing surgery is often laden with inconveniences and burdens. In this track, we encourage participants to identify and propose solutions to improve the overall patient experience with surgery.
