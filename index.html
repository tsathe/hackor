A 24-hour event that brings together individuals from all disciplines and all levels of training to brainstorm solutions to challenges in surgery and pitch for prizes.


INNOVATE. PROTOTYPE.  REPEAT. 

A hackathon is an event in which people at all levels of training (attendings, residents, medical students), and across variety of disciplines (medicine, engineering, design, etc.) come together to come up with problems in a particular field and design solutions through iterative prototyping.

We want to apply this concept to the field of surgery. We believe in a one day event, teams will be able to come up with amazing concepts that can lay the groundwork for future projects and ventures. Prototypes can be simple sketches or mock ups. The goal is to iterate and improve rapidly in a short period of time, so the prototype doesn't have to be perfect.

For more information, you can find additional resources here.

TRACK ONE

PATIENT  SAFETY 

There is growing need globaly to design operations, workflows and maneuvers that reduce the risk of medical errors. Complications after surgery are among some of the greatest costs to healthcare and can incur tremendous emotional, financial and physical burden on patients and their families. We have an obligation to work towards mitigating these issues as much as possible.

TRACK TWO

IMPROVE  OUTCOMES 

Technological advancements have given way to outstanding developments in the field of surgery across many specialties and conditions. Still, there are some conditions for which surgical interventions are currently limited. There is incredible potential to adapt mechanical, computational and imaging technologies to expand the capabilities of surgical teams to reduce suffering.

TRACK THREE

RETAINING  STAFF 

The demanding needs of surgery can be taxing for surgical teams and staff. Creative strategies to improve structures and reduce staff burn-out is essential to building resilience and improving hospital culture and teamwork, which ultimately benefit patients through the care that they receive.
