INNOVATE. PROTOTYPE.  REPEAT. 

A hackathon is an event in which people at all levels of training (attendings, residents, medical students), and across variety of disciplines (medicine, engineering, design, etc.) come together to come up with problems in a particular field and design solutions through iterative prototyping.

We want to apply this concept to the field of surgery. We believe in a one day event, teams will be able to come up with amazing concepts that can lay the groundwork for future projects and ventures. Prototypes can be simple sketches or mock ups. The goal is to iterate and improve rapidly in a short period of time, so the prototype doesn't have to be perfect.

For more information, you can find additional resources here.

TRACK ONE

DEVICE  DESIGN 

One of the essential challenges in surgery is to design instruments that are effective, durable, biologically compatible, and practical. Whether it is making precise incisions, controlling bleeding, improving visual fields, or connecting tissue, there are a wide-variety of tasks that must be accomplished during an operation. In this track, we encourage participants to form novel ideas for solutions that could solve common challenges faced within the operating room.

Prize 1

TRACK TWO

PATIENT  EXPERIENCE 

A key component of the surgical experience takes place outside of the operating room. From clinical diagnosis, to hospital admission, and ultimately follow up, the physical and emotional process of undergoing surgery is often laden with inconveniences and burdens. In this track, we encourage participants to identify problems and propose solutions to improve the overall patient surgical experience.

Prize 2
